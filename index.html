About Me

My name is Yalun Wen. I am a Ph.D. student of mechanical engineering at Texas A&M University, supervised by Dr. Prabhakar Pagilla. My research focuses on the use of differential geometry and control theory to achieve planning and control of robot manipulators that can simultaneously travel with constant speed and achieve the pre-assigned orientation along a spatial curve. I had internships with Flexiv robotics and Honda Research Institute. My motion and force control strategies have been tested and employed by Lockheed Martin via collaboration with the ARM. I have received the TAMU MEEN scholarship, research excellence fellowship, and the AFS scholarship.

I have been actively involved with robot motion control for 4.5 years. My involvement with robot control requires the domain knowledge of linear/nonlinear/adaptive control, numerical optimal control (direct method). I’m familiar with velocity-actuated, torque-actuated, and kinematically redundant robot manipulators. The manipulators I had experience with are UR5, UR10e, UR16e, ABB4600, KUKA IIWA, Panda, Flexiv Rizon. My expected graduation is May 2022, and I’m open to robotics control engineer/research scientist/system analyst. On this website, you can find all my current publications as first author and video demos for some of my work.
